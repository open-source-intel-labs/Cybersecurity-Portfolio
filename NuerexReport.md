# OSINT Investigation: Nuerex

OSINT Lab Report: Investigating Suspicious Consulting Websites
========================================================


Date: 11/6/25
Focus Area: Cybersecurity / OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)
Objective: Investigate the legitimacy of a job posting and the associated company website using OSINT techniques.

--------------------------------------------------------
Objective
--------------------------------------------------------
Evaluate the credibility of a consulting firm (“Nuerex”) https://www.nuerex.com discovered through a job posting on Indeed. Determine whether the site is legitimate or potentially fraudulent by analyzing its content, structure, and external signals.

--------------------------------------------------------
Methodology
--------------------------------------------------------
1. Website Content Analysis
   - Reviewed “About” and “Industry Expertise” pages.
   - Identified vague, boilerplate language and unrealistic industry breadth.
   - Noted typos such as “GOVERMENT” and odd phrasing like “Human resources are our greatest recourse.”

2. Reverse Image Search
   - Conducted reverse image search on a diagram from Nuerex’s site.
   - Discovered identical content on another site: AgilityPartnersLLC.com.

3. Text Comparison
   - Compared blog posts and About pages across both sites.
   - Found word‑for‑word duplication (e.g., “Misconceptions and Misinformation about Machine Learning consultants…”).

4. Domain/WHOIS Check
   - Observed both domains were registered recently (2020–2021).
   - Ownership details masked with privacy protection.

5. Social Media Link Verification
   - Found Facebook/LinkedIn icons loop back to the homepage.
   - No evidence of real social presence.

--------------------------------------------------------
Findings
--------------------------------------------------------
- Duplicate content across both sites.
- Typos and errors replicated (“recourse” vs. “resource,” “GOVERMENT”).
- Privacy‑protected domains, no staff bios, no careers page.
- Suspicious job ads posted on Indeed without a legitimate hiring portal.
- Sites look visually different but contain the same filler content.

--------------------------------------------------------
Analysis
--------------------------------------------------------
- Both sites appear to be shell websites or clones, likely generated from templates or AI‑written filler.
- The probable end goal is harvesting job applications and personal data under the guise of consulting firms.
- This investigation demonstrates practical OSINT techniques: reverse image search, WHOIS lookup, text analysis, and credibility assessment.

--------------------------------------------------------
Conclusion
--------------------------------------------------------
The investigation strongly suggests that Nuerex and Agility Partners LLC are not legitimate consulting firms. Instead, they are part of a network of clone sites designed to appear professional while lacking substance. Their likely purpose is to lure job seekers and collect personal information.

--------------------------------------------------------
Skills Demonstrated
--------------------------------------------------------
- OSINT research (reverse image search, WHOIS, text fingerprinting)
- Critical analysis of web content and credibility signals
- Cybersecurity mindset: identifying potential fraud/scam patterns
- Documentation of findings in a structured lab format
