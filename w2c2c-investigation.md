# OSINT Investigation: w2c2c

OSINT Case Study: Investigating W2c2c.com

Date: February 2026
Objective: Assess credibility of W2c2c.com as a staffing/recruitment portal using OSINT techniques.

Executive Summary
A job posting for a “Cybersecurity Specialist” was discovered on Indeed.com, attributed to W2c2c PVT LTD. Through OSINT analysis, multiple red flags were identified: mismatched job descriptions, pirated software content, fabricated employer listings, fake candidate profiles, reliance on a personal Facebook profile as its corporate presence, and technical anomalies in domain registration. Findings suggest W2c2c.com is not a legitimate recruiter but rather a resume‑harvesting or fraudulent portal.
This investigation demonstrates the ability to apply OSINT methods to detect deception, assess credibility, and protect sensitive information.

Artifact 1: Suspicious Job Posting (Indeed.com)
Source: Indeed.com listing for Cybersecurity Specialist by W2c2c PVT LTD. 
Description: Responsibilities aligned with Data Analyst (ETL, Talend, Visio). 
Issues:
• Title/description mismatch.
• Vague language (“we will submit your profile”).
• Contradictory relocation requirement for remote role.
• Wide pay range ($35–$85/hr).
Conclusion: Even though the posting appeared on a legitimate job board (Indeed), the underlying recruiter was questionable. This demonstrates how fraudulent postings can infiltrate trusted platforms. 

Artifact 2: Website Content
Blog section: Hosted “Free Microsoft Office Professional Plus 2024 Download” with pirated software links and password‑protected installers. 
Issues:
• Unrelated to staffing.
• Security risk (malware/phishing).
• Undermines credibility of site.
Conclusion: Hosting cracked software confirms site is unsafe and unprofessional. 

Artifact 3: Employer and Candidate Listings
Employer example: “Figma Featured.”
• Contact info: Fake phone number (“123 456 789”), fake email (“figma@fakeuser.com”).
• Description: Copy‑pasted text about Moody’s Corporation from public sources.
• Issues: Identity mismatch, fabricated contact details, no jobs listed.
Candidate example: “setcleartargets1984.”
• About section: Text about CBD, vaping, THC pens, alcohol vaporizers.
• Issues: Irrelevant content, likely scraped from lifestyle blogs, no résumé or professional data.
Conclusion: Both employer and candidate sections are populated with fake or irrelevant content to simulate legitimacy and activity.

Artifact 4: Personal Facebook Link
Linked profile: “Naveen Sai Rockstar.” 
Bio: “USA W2c2c.com | Writer, Artist, Traveler, Foodie.” 
Followers: 4.2K. 
Issues:
• Personal account used as corporate presence.
• Lifestyle branding unrelated to staffing.
• Follower count may be inflated or unrelated to recruiting.
Conclusion: Corporate identity overlaps with personal branding, undermining credibility. 

Artifact 5: WHOIS/DNS Analysis
Domain registered: January 14, 2024; expires January 14, 2027. 
Registrar: GoDaddy.com, LLC. 
Name servers: ns1.bluehost.in, ns2.bluehost.in (Bluehost India). 
Status: Standard registrar locks (delete/renew/transfer/update prohibited). 
Abuse contact: GoDaddy abuse email/phone.
Issues:
• Domain is newly registered (2024), inconsistent with claims of being an established recruiter.
• Hosting points to Bluehost India, contradicting claims of U.S. presence.
• Registrant details masked, reducing transparency.
Conclusion: Technical records show the site is newly registered, hosted outside the U.S., and lacks transparency — inconsistent with a legitimate staffing firm. 

Overall Findings
W2c2c.com exhibits multiple indicators of fraud:
• Resume harvesting language.
• Pirated software content.
• Fake employer and candidate profiles.
• Reliance on personal social media for corporate identity.
• Domain registered recently, hosted outside the U.S., with privacy masking. 
Not a legitimate staffing firm. 
Unsafe to submit personal information.
